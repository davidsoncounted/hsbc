Managing your foreign currency

The HSBC Currency Account is an easy-to-manage currency holding account, with no monthly account fee. You can choose from 14 major currencies and open a separate account for each one to store, send and receive money. 

Designed to support your international lifestyle, with easy access to your account via online banking, you can manage your finances wherever you are in the world.

Account features:

  • No account fee
    There's no monthly fee for the account and no payment fee when sending money to another HSBC account anywhere in the world. You can also send money to non HSBC accounts outside the UK fees may apply.
  • Live exchange rates
    With live rates updated by the second during market hours, we'll provide you with our most up-to-date foreign exchange rate available when sending money.
  • Managing your payments
    You can create immediate payments to send money outside the UK, or set these up online up to a year in advance or on a recurring basis.

How to apply for a HSBC Currency Account

Are you eligible?

 

To apply for an HSBC Currency Account you need to:

  • hold an active HSBC current account (excluding Basic Bank Account)
  • be 18 years of age or over

If you're not yet an HSBC customer, find out more about our current accounts.

Apply online in minutes

If you’re an existing HSBC current account (excluding Basic Bank Account) customer, log on or register for online banking to apply.

More information

The rate we use is the HSBC Exchange Rate at the time your payment is made. This is a live rate which is updated by the second during market hours. We’ll notify you each time you make a payment from your account. Where a currency exchange has been involved, we’ll confirm the HSBC Exchange Rate that has been applied.

It’s not possible to cancel an immediate payment or transfer. If you need to cancel a payment that is due to be made on a future or recurring date, then you have until 23:59 (UK time) the day before the payment is due, to cancel it. This can only be done via online banking.

Please note: there’s no interest payable on the HSBC Currency Account.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a currency account? 

A currency account is a way to store, send and receive multiple currencies.

The HSBC Currency Account is a way of managing money in foreign currency in a separate account.

You can:

  • Choose from 14 currencies
  • Open an account for each currency
  • Send money to another HSBC account anywhere in the world without paying any fees

Fees may apply if you send money to a non-HSBC account outside the UK.

How can I pay into or withdraw funds from my account? 

  • You can pay into your HSBC Currency Account by making a transfer from your HSBC sterling account, or from another bank account. You can do this online, in branch or over the phone. You can find your account details on your welcome letter and on your statement
  • You can withdraw money from your HSBC Currency Account by transferring money to your HSBC sterling account. You can do this online, in branch or over the phone
  • Payments from your account can be arranged using our International Payment Service
  • To pay in or withdraw cash, please visit a full service or cash service HSBC branch. For more information see Branch banking.

Please note:

  • This account doesn’t have overdraft facilities. Please make sure you have sufficient funds in your account before making a payment or withdrawal
  • We’ll be carrying out system maintenance from Saturday 22:00 – Sunday 08:00 (UK time). You may only have limited access to your HSBC Currency Account(s) during this time, if you’re accessing it through online banking
  • During this maintenance period, you’ll be able to view your account but the balances and transactions may not be up to date. It also won’t be possible to make transfers to or from your HSBC Currency Account(s), or make payments from it

How much can I send and how long will it take? 

International payments:

Online: up to £50,000 (or currency equivalent)

In branch: unlimited

Over the phone: unlimited for Premier customers, and up to £10,000 (or currency equivalent) for non-Premier customers

 

Transfers between your own HSBC accounts:

Online: up to £250,000 (or currency equivalent)

In branch: unlimited

Over the phone: unlimited

Note: transfers and international payments may not leave your account immediately. There’s no need to repeat the payments as you risk multiple debits from your account. 

Payments to accounts in the EEA in euro and sterling will normally be credited the next working day*. All other payments will normally take up to 4 working days* (but may take longer depending on the country the money is being sent to).

* subject to currency cut-off time and further checks, as from time to time we may need to conduct additional security checks to validate the payment.

Why can’t I see my HSBC Currency Account when I log on to online banking? It seems to have vanished. 

If there have been no transactions in or out of your HSBC Currency Account for 24 months, it’s marked as ‘dormant’.

At this point it will disappear from your list of accounts in online banking.

If you see your account has gone dormant, please call us on 03457 404 404 or visit your local branch. We can reactivate your HCAs or transfer any remaining funds into another account. 

How can I stop my HSBC Currency Account going dormant?  

You can prevent your HSBC Currency Account from going dormant by making a transfer out of it at least twice a year to any other account.

It will then continue to appear in your list of accounts in online banking.

If it’s become dormant but you still want to use the account, simply call us on 03457 404 404 or visit your local branch. We can reactivate the account.
